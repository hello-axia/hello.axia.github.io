Your Vote Matters

By Axia Staff • Aug 15, 2025

Every time to perform our civic duty, we might look at the ballot and think “Does my vote really matter? One more vote won’t change anything.” And honestly, I get it. Politics does often feel overwhelming. It’s like a black box, something we don’t completely understand, and something that feels like it has little effect on our lives. But here is the truth: our lives are being shaped by what we vote for.

Why your vote matters

The reality is, older voters turn out in much higher numbers. For example, only 13% of Americans aged 18–29 voted in the midterms, a historic low that left youth interest largely unattended. When we don’t show up, we hand over our future. But when younger people do turn out, we change everything. In 2018 and 2022, we shocked the experts and even shifted the balance in key races.

You might think that “my one vote doesn’t matter,” but elections are literally decided by thin margins— sometimes a few hundred votes in a district decide what happens. That one person staying home makes a difference.

Should you care?

Now the question is: should you care? Let me tell you, I think you should. What is on the ballot isn’t abstract. It's also not just the political figures who are running that you are voting on. It’s your everyday life.

  • Minimum Wage Increases (how much you make)
  • Rent Control Measures (how much rent costs)
  • School Funding Bonds (the quality of education)
  • Medicaid Expansions (how much insurance you pay)
  • Marijuana Legalization (if you can legally smoke)
  • Gun Safety Laws (who should own guns)

What ballot language looks like

And the voting is very straightforward on these ballots. For prompts like these, you simply answer “Yes” or “No.”

Minimum Wage Increase

“Raises minimum wage to $10.00 per hour effective September 30, 2021… reaches $15.00 per hour on September 30, 2026… future increases adjusted annually for inflation.”
  • Step-up to $15 by 2026
  • Then indexed to inflation

Rent Control Authority

“Expands local governments’ authority to enact rent control… repeals Costa-Hawkins… allows limits on any housing, including single-family homes and post-1995 apartments… allows limits between tenancies.”
  • Broader local power to cap rents
  • Applies to more types/years of housing

K-12 & Community College Bonds

“Authorizing bonds for public schools and community college facilities… $10 billion for repairs and new construction… with annual audits.”
  • $10B for facilities
  • Audit and oversight required

Every single election, these choices are being made. And when you don’t show up, someone else makes them for you.

Bottom line

Here’s the bottom line: voting isn’t just a duty. It’s your right. It’s saying: I’m here, I care, and I deserve a say in the world I’ll inherit. Every ballot is a direct stake in decisions that shape your daily life. From how much you pay for college to whether your neighborhood gets affordable housing, your single vote matters when these decisions hinge on a couple hundred votes.
